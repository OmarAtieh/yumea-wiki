## Key Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Dreamscape** | The board where tiles are placed |
| **Persona** | Your character with unique abilities |
| **Harmony** | Points earned by connecting matching tiles |
| **Tension** | Threat level; nightmares emerge at thresholds |
| **Nightmare** | Card-based threats that block tiles |
| **Anchor** | Pattern-based objective; complete 3 to win |
| **Purification** | Removing nightmares using cards |
| **Corruption** | Permanent damage if nightmares stay too long |
| **Energy** | Resource spent to use abilities |

## Winning vs. Losing

**Win by:**
- Completing 3 Anchors (pattern objectives)

**Lose if:**
- Tension reaches 20
- All personas at 0 energy simultaneously
- 5+ tiles corrupted

## Game Modes

| Mode | Changes | Best For |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Family** | No Reverberations, slower corruption, 4 starting energy | Ages 8+, first games |
| **Standard** | Full rules as written | Regular play |
| **Advanced** | Faster corruption, harder anchors, solo Nightmare AI | Experienced players |

## Comparison to Similar Games

| Game | Similarity | Yumea's Difference |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Spirit Island** | Cooperative, escalating threat | Emotional theme, tile-building vs. area control |
| **Cascadia** | Tile placement, pattern scoring | Cooperative not competitive, narrative arc |
| **Dixit** | Dream imagery, emotional resonance | Strategic depth, cooperative gameplay |
| **Mysterium** | Dreams, cooperative deduction | Different mechanics, shared victory |
| **Stardew Valley** (board game) | Relaxing, cooperative | More strategic tension, nightmare threat |

**Yumea's unique position:** *Spirit Island* meets *Dixit's* emotional depth, with *Cascadia's* tile elegance.
