### Sacred Core â€” What Makes Yumea Yumea

These five principles are non-negotiable. Every system, every card, every rule must serve at least one:

**1. Emotion as Mechanic**
The game doesn't just *have* emotions â€” it *is* emotions. Tile placement isn't spatial optimization; it's choosing what to build in a psyche. Nightmares aren't enemies to defeat; they're fears to face. Every mechanic must reflect, evoke, or counter a real emotional state.

**2. Cooperative Restoration**
Victory isn't conquest. You don't win by destroying the nightmares â€” you win by restoring harmony. The dreamer heals. The players support. The metric is wholeness, not dominance.

**3. Symbolic Spatial Design**
The board *is* the mind. Where you place matters emotionally, not just mechanically. Proximity is intimacy. Distance is isolation. The spatial relationships carry symbolic weight.

**4. Accessible Depth**
Jenin can play the family mode at age three. A hobbyist can spend forty hours mastering persona synergies. Same game, different depths. The complexity emerges from the system, not the rules.

**5. Mirrored Emotional Axis**
